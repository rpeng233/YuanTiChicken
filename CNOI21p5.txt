Represent each character by a fixed \(2\times2\) matrix, but maintain the sequence in reverse order:

\[
W=\begin{pmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{pmatrix},\qquad
E=\begin{pmatrix}2&-1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}.
\]

For the reversed operation string, let the matrix product be \(M\). Then the required fraction is

\[
\frac{M_{00}}{M_{00}+M_{01}}
\]

taken modulo \(998244353\).

Use an implicit treap/splay to maintain the reversed string. Each node stores:

- forward product of its interval;
- backward product of its interval;
- the same two products after flipping all characters.

Lazy tags:

- `rev`: swap children and swap forward/backward products;
- `flip`: swap normal/flipped products and toggle the character.

Operations become:

- append a character: insert it at the front of the maintained reversed sequence;
- flip \([l,r]\): convert to \([n-r+1,n-l+1]\), split it out, apply `flip`;
- reverse \([l,r]\): convert the same way, split it out, apply `rev`.

After each update, take the root’s forward product \(M\), output \(M_{00}\) and \(M_{00}+M_{01}\). Matrix multiplication and all values are modulo \(998244353\). The total complexity is \(O((n+q)\log(n+q))\).
