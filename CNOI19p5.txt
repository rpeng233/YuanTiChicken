Observe that one operation with parameter \(A\) is a uniformly random interleaving of two ordered parts. Thus, for any position \(x\), the previous position contributing to it follows a hypergeometric distribution.

Maintain the expectation function as a polynomial in \(x\). Since the initial value is either linear or quadratic, and the above transition preserves degree, it is enough to keep coefficients in the basis

\[
1,\quad y=x-1,\quad \binom{y}{2}.
\]

Initial coefficients are:

\[
x:\ (1,1,0),\qquad x^2:\ (1,3,2).
\]

For each operation, suppose the current polynomial is

\[
c_0+c_1y+c_2\binom{y}{2}.
\]

Let \(D=\binom nA\), and treat invalid binomial coefficients as \(0\). Compute

\[
p_1=
\frac{
A\binom{n-1}{A}
+
\left(\binom{n-2}{A-2}+\binom{n-2}{A}\right)y
}{D},
\]

\[
p_2=
\frac{
\frac{A(A-1)}2\binom{n-1}{A}
+
A\binom{n-2}{A}y
+
\left(\binom{n-3}{A-3}+\binom{n-3}{A}\right)\binom{y}{2}
}{D}.
\]

Then replace the polynomial by

\[
c_0+c_1p_1+c_2p_2.
\]

Precompute factorials and inverse factorials to answer every binomial coefficient in \(O(1)\). After processing all operations, answer a query position \(x\) by evaluating

\[
c_0+c_1(x-1)+c_2\binom{x-1}{2}.
\]

Overall complexity is \(O(n+m+q)\), with \(O(n)\) preprocessing memory.
