Key Idea

For a connected component $C$ remaining after the deletions,

\max_{s_C\in{-1,+1}}
s_C\sum_{i\in C} B_i.
$$

Therefore, assign every remaining component a sign $+1$ or $-1$. Equivalently, assign every vertex one of three states:

+: the vertex remains and belongs to a positive component;
deleted: the vertex is removed;
-: the vertex remains and belongs to a negative component.

Two adjacent vertices cannot have states + and -, since retained adjacent vertices must lie in the same component and hence have the same sign. This is the transformation used by the official editorial.

Converting Profit into Cost

Let

$$
BASE=\sum_{i=1}^{N}|B_i|.
$$

For each vertex, measure the loss relative to receiving its maximum possible contribution $|B_i|$.

If $B_i\geq 0$:

State	Contribution to profit	Loss
+	$B_i$	$0$
deleted	$-A_i$	$B_i+A_i$
-	$-B_i$	$2B_i$

If $B_i<0$:

State	Contribution to profit	Loss
+	$B_i=-	B_i	$	$2	B_i	$
deleted	$-A_i$	$	B_i	+A_i$
-	$-B_i=	B_i	$	$0$

Denote these three losses by

$$
c_i(+),\qquad c_i(D),\qquad c_i(-).
$$

The answer is then

$$
BASE-\min(\text{total loss}),
$$

subject to the condition that no original edge has one endpoint labeled + and the other labeled -.

Minimum-Cut Construction

Create a source $S$, a sink $T$, and two vertices $X_i,Y_i$ for every original vertex $i$.

Interpret their positions in an $S$-$T$ cut as follows:

$X_i$ side	$Y_i$ side	State
$S$	$S$	+
$S$	$T$	deleted
$T$	$T$	-

Add the edge

$$
Y_i\rightarrow X_i
$$

with infinite capacity. This forbids the fourth configuration $(X_i,Y_i)=(T,S)$.

To charge the three state costs, add:

$Y_i\rightarrow T$ with capacity $c_i(+)$;
$X_i\rightarrow Y_i$ with capacity $c_i(D)$;
$S\rightarrow X_i$ with capacity $c_i(-)$.

Exactly one of these finite edges crosses the cut, depending on the chosen state.

For every original undirected edge ${u,v}$, add

$$
Y_u\rightarrow X_v,\qquad Y_v\rightarrow X_u
$$

with infinite capacity.

If $u$ is + and $v$ is -, then $Y_u$ lies on the source side while $X_v$ lies on the sink side, so an infinite-capacity edge is cut. The reverse edge similarly forbids $u$ being - and $v$ being +.

Thus, every finite cut corresponds exactly to a valid assignment, and its capacity equals the total loss.

Final Answer

Compute the maximum flow, which equals the minimum cut:

\sum_{i=1}^{N}|B_i|

\operatorname{maxflow}(S,T)
}.
$$

Choose INF larger than the sum of all finite capacities, for example $2^{60}$ when using 64-bit integers.

Correctness Summary
The absolute value of each component sum can be represented by choosing a sign for that component.
This gives the three vertex states +, deleted, and -, with + and - forbidden across an edge.
The two-node gadget represents exactly these three states.
Its finite cut capacity equals the loss contributed by the selected state.
Infinite edges enforce all validity constraints.
Hence the minimum cut gives the minimum loss, and subtracting it from $\sum |B_i|$ gives the maximum profit.
Complexity

The flow graph has:

$2N+2=O(N)$ vertices;
$O(N+M)$ edges.

Using Dinic's algorithm, the complexity is

$$
O\left(N^2(N+M)\right),
$$

which is sufficient for $N,M\leq 300$.
