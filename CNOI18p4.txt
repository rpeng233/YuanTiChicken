First simulate the deterministic choices. Maintain the current values in a multiset; at step \(i\), take the largest value \(\le a_i\), or the minimum value if none exists. Record it as \(k_i\), erase it, then insert the new value gained after this step.

For every step, the fixed integer \(x\) must satisfy two conditions:

\[
k_i x \ge a_i
\]

and

\[
k_i x \equiv a_i \pmod {p_i}.
\]

The inequalities only give a global lower bound:

\[
L=\max_i \left\lceil \frac{a_i}{k_i} \right\rceil.
\]

Now solve the congruences. For each

\[
k_i x \equiv a_i \pmod {p_i},
\]

let

\[
g=\gcd(k_i,p_i).
\]

If \(a_i\not\equiv 0\pmod g\), there is no solution. Otherwise divide by \(g\):

\[
\frac{k_i}{g}x \equiv \frac{a_i}{g}\pmod{\frac{p_i}{g}}.
\]

Since \(\frac{k_i}{g}\) and \(\frac{p_i}{g}\) are coprime, use extended Euclid to get its inverse and convert it into

\[
x \equiv r_i \pmod {q_i}.
\]

Combine all such congruences with extended CRT. Maintain

\[
x \equiv ans \pmod M.
\]

To merge with

\[
x \equiv r \pmod q,
\]

solve

\[
ans+Mt \equiv r \pmod q.
\]

Let \(d=\gcd(M,q)\). If \(r-ans\) is not divisible by \(d\), there is no solution. Otherwise reduce and solve for \(t\) modulo \(q/d\), then update

\[
ans \leftarrow ans+Mt,
\qquad
M \leftarrow \operatorname{lcm}(M,q).
\]

Normalize \(ans\) into \([0,M)\).

After all congruences are merged, choose the smallest number congruent to \(ans\) modulo \(M\) and at least \(L\):

\[
ans += \left\lceil \frac{L-ans}{M} \right\rceil M
\]

if \(ans<L\).

Use 128-bit integers for intermediate multiplication. The total complexity is

\[
O((n+m)\log m+n\log V).
\]
